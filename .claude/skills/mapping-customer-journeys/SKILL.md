# Mapping Customer Journeys

## Overview

A journey map visualizes the **process a person goes through to accomplish a goal** — a chronological narrative enriched with thoughts, emotions, and pain points, used to build shared understanding and locate opportunities. Its real value is the cross-functional **conversation and alignment** it forces, not the poster.

## Choose the artifact

| Artifact | Scope | Use when |
|----------|-------|----------|
| **Journey map** | Specific product/business, one persona | Understand & align on a known experience, find pain points |
| **Experience map** | Generic, product-agnostic | Understand general human behavior toward a goal (often a precursor) |
| **Service blueprint** | The organization delivering the service | Omnichannel / cross-functional services — map what's behind the touchpoints |

## Journey map structure (three zones)

- **Zone A — Lens:** one **actor** (a persona/POV) + a **scenario** with goals/expectations.
- **Zone B — Experience:** **phases** across the top, and swim lanes for **actions**, **thoughts/mindsets** (ideally verbatim quotes), and a single **emotion curve** showing ups and downs. Pain points surface where emotion dips.
- **Zone C — Insights:** **opportunities** to improve, with **ownership** and metrics.

## Service blueprint structure

The "sequel" to a journey map — same customer-actions lane on top, then organizational lanes split by three lines:
- **Customer actions** → **Line of interaction** → **Frontstage** (visible to customer) → **Line of visibility** → **Backstage** (hidden) → **Line of internal interaction** → **Support processes**. Plus **physical/digital evidence** per touchpoint. Every backstage/support step should trace to a customer-visible touchpoint.

## Workflow

1. Pick the artifact for the question and audience.
2. Choose one actor + one scenario (don't blend personas).
3. Ground every cell in research; if you must start from assumptions, label it a **hypothesis map** and plan validation.
4. Map phases → actions → thoughts → emotion curve → pain points.
5. Convert pain points into prioritized opportunities with owners.
6. Escalate to a service blueprint when the experience is omnichannel or cross-departmental.

## Quality bar

- [ ] One actor + one scenario per map.
- [ ] Actions/thoughts/emotions are research-grounded (or the map is labeled a **hypothesis map** that lists its top unvalidated assumptions and the next research step before it's shared).
- [ ] Pain points are converted into owned opportunities.
- [ ] Blueprint backstage/support steps each tie to a visible touchpoint.
